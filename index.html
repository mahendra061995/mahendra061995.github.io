About Me

Atmospheric scientist in the making

I am a third-year PhD research scholar at the Centre for Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (CAOS), Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. My research focuses on understanding the complex dynamics of Earth's atmosphere and oceans, particularly over the Indian subcontinent.

I am passionate about leveraging observational data, reanalysis products, and numerical simulations to unravel the mechanisms governing monsoon variability, extreme weather events, and climate-scale phenomena.

Research

Current interests and themes

🌧

Monsoon Variability

Investigating intra-seasonal, inter-annual and decadal variability in the Indian Summer Monsoon and its teleconnections.

🌊

Ocean–Atmosphere Coupling

Understanding how sea surface temperature anomalies in the Indian Ocean influence large-scale circulation patterns.

🌀

Extreme Weather Events

Studying the mechanisms, predictability, and impacts of extreme precipitation and heat events over South Asia.

🛰

Observational Analysis

Using satellite data, reanalysis products (ERA5, MERRA-2), and station observations for diagnostic studies.
